Why I Started Automating? 

Everywhere I went, the biggest request was always the same:

“Can you automate onboarding and offboarding?”

It didn’t matter what company, what team, or what tech stack—people were drowning in repetitive provisioning steps, manual checklists, and error‑prone handoffs. And honestly, I felt that pain too. Watching people spend hours clicking through the same screens over and over made it obvious: there had to be a better way.
That’s where this page comes in.

This is the place where my love for automation really started.

At first, it was just a collection of small scripts—rough, functional, and built to solve immediate problems. They weren’t elegant, but they worked. They saved time. They reduced mistakes. And they opened the door to something bigger.
Over time, those early scripts evolved into full workflows:
- Automated hardware provisioning
- Dynamic ID resolution
- Onboarding sequences that created accounts, assigned assets, and set people up for success
- Offboarding flows that locked accounts, reclaimed hardware, and triggered downstream tasks
- Logging, auditing, and error‑handling that made everything production‑ready

This page became a kind of personal archive—a reminder of where I started and how far the automation mindset can take you. I’ve kept the old scripts here on purpose. They’re not perfect, but they’re honest. They show the progression from “just make this easier” to “let’s build something scalable, reliable, and elegant.”
Automation didn’t just solve problems for other people.
It reshaped how I think, how I build, and how I approach every challenge.
And it all started with onboarding and offboarding
